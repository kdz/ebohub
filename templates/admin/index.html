Trying It Out

Text #help to 512-942-0915 to get started

Here is one possible scenario you could try

  • Gather 2 or more cell phones. Let's call 2 of these phones PATIENT and NURSE.
  • Point your web browser at EboHub
  • Note the primary contact number for all Ebohub SMS and Voice interactions. Let's call this EBONUMBER.
  • Browse the list of current patients, chiefdoms (regions), and health care workers.
  • PATIENT sends #help to EBONUMBER to see SMS options as a patient.
  • PATIENT sends #info and receives information to self-check symptoms etc.
  • PATIENT places a voice call to EBONUMBER and discusses his issues. Voice-reponse is just a placeholder for now.
  • PATIENT registers as a Patient by sending i'm sick to EBONUMBER.
  • PATIENT registers his name by sending name terrence.
  • NURSE registers as a health-care worker in Yorkville by:
    • Creating an entry on the H C Worker tab / page
    • Register the phone number in this exact form: +1 followed by area-code and number, without any extra characters.
  • NURSE asks for her assigned tasks by sending todo. She receives assigned patient information (id and contact) in her current location.
  • PATIENT registers moves into Yorkville by sending loc Yorkville. NURSE is notified with patient-id and contact info since she is in Yorkville.
  • NURSE visits PATIENT and sends update with patient-id and status.
  • NURSE gets infected and sends in i'm sick.
  • An administrator sorts the tables by different columns to get overviews.
  • The administrator visits the Logs and Map page to look at the current outbreak map.

Info about Ebola

The 2014 Ebola epidemic is the largest in history, affecting multiple countries in West Africa.

This service uses primarily SMS-based communication to help track, update, and co-ordinate patients, health care workers, and administrators in the battle against Ebola. EboHub helps:
  • Provide Ebola-related information to anyone in the population
  • Identify potentially infected people
  • Reassure uninfected people about symptoms
  • Connect infected people with health care workers or centers
  • Trace the contacts of infected or potentially infected people
  • Manage the cycle of symptoms, quarantine, and isolation
